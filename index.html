Subhash Chandra Bose

The Indian Nationalist Leader

Here's a time line of Subhash Chandra Bose

  • Born on January 23, 1897 in Cuttack, Odisha, India.
  • Ninth child of Janakinath Bose, a lawyer, and Prabhavati Devi.
  • Educated at the Protestant European School, Cuttack.
  • University of Calcutta, where he studied philosophy.
  • Joined the Indian National Congress and quickly rose through the ranks to become one of its leaders.
  • Strong advocate for Indian independence and was arrested multiple times for his activism.
  • In 1942, Bose formed the Indian National Army (INA) with the goal of freeing India from British rule.
  • The INA fought alongside the Japanese in the Burma campaign during World War II.
  • Bose died in a plane crash on August 18, 1945.
  • Remembered as a hero in India for his role in the Indian independence movement.

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom."

---Subhash Chandra Bose
